Project / Assignment

Project Meeting Notes & Action Item Assistant GPT

A custom mini-agent created to organize messy meeting notes and transcripts into clear summaries and action items.

Problem

During meetings there is usually a lot going on at once. People are talking through ideas, making decisions, and figuring out what tasks need to be complete. With all that in mind, it can be easy to forget all the small things that were mentioned or get sidetrack on what to do. This mini-agent helps organize messy notes or transcripts into a simple summary with clear action items so nothing gets missed.

Who Would Use It

Students would mainly use this type of mini-agent, especially ones working in group projects. This is because conversations tend to be scattered and not always documented clearly. This tool could also be useful for professionals working on teams projects or to give to a member that needs to be brought up to speed on something quickly.

Context the Mini-Agent Needs

This mini-agent should understand how meetings and group discussions usually work. It should also recognize when tasks or roles are assigned, and when questions or follow-ups come up. Another thing it needs to understand that meeting notes might be messy or incomplete. Its job it to organize the information without adding new details.

Expected Outcome

The mini-agent should produce a clear meeting summary that includes the main discussion points, key decisions, action items, and any follow-up questions. The goal is to make it easy for a team to quickly understand what happened in the meeting and what tasks need to be completed next.

Testing Reflection

The mini-agent did well when organizing messy discussion into a structure format. I even gave it a Teams chat to see what it could do. It was able to identify action items and discussion points. The follow up questions were not always needed but were nice if there was a second meeting. It even did well will transcripts and labeling who spoke about what. One strength of the tool was that it did not invent missing deadlines or filler information that was not explicitly discussed.

However, I do think it missed deadlines just because wording was off or GPTs do not really do well with time. For example, if someone says this needs to be done by tomorrow, the GPT will not give an exact date back but say tomorrow.

Ethical Concern

One ethical concern I have with this is that the mini-agent could summarize or organize information incorrectly or share information that was not meant to be public.
